Four options. Custom HTML (A1, A2) vs. iframe embed (B1, B2).

For the custom forms, the visual and the backend are independent choices — either A1 or A2 can be wired to Formspree (~$10/mo at peak) or to a Google Form backend (free, slightly hacky).

The Google Form mocks below are static recreations — they don't actually submit. The real iframe would behave identically but go to Sheets.

THINKING NOTES

A1 vs A2: A1 is one scroll, faster for guests. A2 feels more crafted but doubles the perceived form length — older guests may lose patience.

B1 vs B2: B2 mitigates the aesthetic break without solving it. The form itself is still Google. Some guests will trust it more because they recognize it.

Hybrid worth considering: custom pixel-art form on rsvp.html, plus a "having trouble? RSVP by email" line for guests who can't deal with a web form. Belt and suspenders.
